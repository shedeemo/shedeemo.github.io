About me

Hello! My name is Mohamed and I'm a Political Science PhD candidate at The Ohio State University and Research Fellow with the Mershon Center for International Security. I am proudly from Jersey City, NJ (home to the Statue of Liberty btw) but moved to Ohio for graduate school. As an undergraduate, I attended Seton Hall University, where I received my degree in International Relations and Economics and worked as a research assistant for the Center for Peace and Conflict Studies.

My substantive asks how demographic factors interact with corruption and clientelism to result in unequal experiences of climate change for citizens of different social groups. I do this by studying the impact of the distribution of Jordan's tribes on both the enforcement of water sector reforms and the reliability of water delivery. My research ties together previous work on corruption, goods provision, and clientelism to understand how social and political factors influence how different populations experience the effects of climate change. Methodologically, I am interested in strategies of causal inference and the application of machine learning methods to political science as a means of data collection. As part of my dissertation, I am currently working on a more efficient means of estimating demographic distributions when accurate data is not publically available or easily accessible.

Teaching

POLISC 7552: Generalized Linear Models

Spring 2023

Teaching Assistant

for Skyler Cranmer

This course is the second in a sequence of three courses designed for the methods sequence of the Political Science PhD program at Ohio State. It covers the theoretical and applied aspects of linear regression and maximum likelihood estimation, with an emphasis on learning how to interpret and evaluate these models for both inferential and predictive purposes

My responsibilities in this course included designing weekly recitation sessions and assisting students with their weekly problem sets and understanding of the material, aside from general TA duties such as grading.

The R code used for recitation sessions can be found in the R Tutorials section.
