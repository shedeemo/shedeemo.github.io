About me

Hello! My name is Mohamed and I'm a Political Science PhD candidate at The Ohio State University with specializations in Comparative Politics and Research Methodology. I am proudly from Jersey City, NJ (home to the Statue of Liberty btw) but moved to Ohio for graduate school. As an undergraduate, I attended Seton Hall University, where I received my degree in International Relations and Economics and worked as a research assistant for the Center for Peace and Conflict Studies.

My substantive interests currently revolve and public goods provision and how demographic factors and corruption result in unequal experiences of climate change for citizens of different social groups. I do this by studying the impact of the distribution of Jordan's tribes on both the enforcement of water sector reforms and the reliability of water delivery. Methodologically, I am interested in strategies of causal inference and the application of machine learning methods to political science as a means of data collection. As part of my dissertation, I am currently working on a more efficient means of estimating demographic distributions when accurate data is not publically available.
